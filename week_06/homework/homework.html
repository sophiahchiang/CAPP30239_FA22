Chart 1: Timeline

I was interested in whether there was any relationship betwen the season and the number of deaths. I was able to examine this trend by graphing number of deaths over time. Consistent with my hypothesis, we see that there is a rise in number of deaths as it gets warmer, during the months of April through May. However because we are only looking at data pulled from 2015 we have no idea whether this is a fluke or whether the trend holds up across multiple years. The decrease we see in the below line graph from September onwards could be a decrease that continues throughout 2016 even as the weather changes.

Chart 2: Age Distribution

This chart illustrates the age distribution of those who were shot in 2015. I chose to use a histogram because it is a concise way of examining this data point. It is easy to tell where the bulk of the people are. Most fall within the ages of 25 and 35. After that, the number of people shot begins to decrease in a fairly linear fashion which is interesting. In other words, it seems that the older you are, the less likely you are to be shot. This correlation is not unexpected. It might also be useful to have an idea of the criminal history of each person included in this data set in order to shed light on why it is that the bulk of people shot fall into the specified age range. Perhaps people in that younger age bracket tend to engage in more illicit or dangerous acitivities which increases the likelihood of them being killed.
